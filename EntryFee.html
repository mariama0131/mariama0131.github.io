Entry Fee



I wash my wounds with my own winds
		––hands and everything
I watch the alcohol as it hits the skin
		––the becoming of the meek 
I water myself with the shade of crimson flowing through my body
		––coloring book for the bleak
I wear my scars on my shoulders
		––a modern soldier’s ranking 

so don’t you dare
think you can barge in
with your sweet talk and soft caresses
your empty bed and busy streets

 			and heal my wounds for me.
